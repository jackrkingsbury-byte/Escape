# The Services Engine — partnering with the friend who already has buyers

**Date:** 21 July 2026
**Trigger:** First real market evidence of the session — Jack's friend builds
websites for businesses, runs their Google/Meta ads, and reports clients
"just pay."

## Why this clears the evidence bar

Every idea so far died on the same rock: no trust channel to buyers. The
friend IS a trust channel. He has demand, closes clients face-to-face, and
gets paid. That's not an idea — it's an operating business with a capacity
problem we can solve.

## The smart version (do this)

**Jack becomes the build engine behind the friend's sales engine.**

- Friend keeps doing what he does: finds clients, sells, owns the relationship.
- Jack (with Claude) builds the actual websites — faster and better than the
  friend can alone. Proof of speed already exists: the StoreBrief landing page
  (design + live demo + working forms) was built in one evening.
- Split: either a flat build fee per site (R1,500–R3,000 depending on size)
  or a 50/50 split of the project price. Flat fee is simpler to start.
- Every client site ships with StoreBrief free as a sweetener the friend can
  sell with ("your site + a weekly WhatsApp of how it's doing") — which also
  makes every client a StoreBrief beta user.

## The trap version (don't do this)

Jack starts a competing agency from scratch. That re-creates the exact
problem that killed direct sales for NeverMiss: a 16-year-old with no
portfolio cold-selling to strangers. The friend's clients pay because they
trust HIM. Don't route around the trust; plug into it.

## What StoreBrief becomes (not killed — backgrounded)

- The Monday routine keeps running automatically (costs nothing).
- Every website client is a StoreBrief prospect with trust pre-built.
- The App Store submission still happens when the Partner account exists.
- The 90-day kill/continue criteria in SHOPIFY-APP-WEDGE.md stand unchanged.

## The audition (next 7 days)

Words are cheap; the friend needs to see the machine run once.

1. Jack sends the friend the proposal message (below).
2. Friend picks ONE real client from his pipeline (or an existing client
   whose site is weak).
3. We build that site in 24–48 hours, end to end.
4. Friend sells it at his usual price; Jack takes the agreed fee.
5. Repeat weekly.

## Money math (sober)

SA small-business sites go for roughly R2,500–R15,000. If the friend closes
2 sites/month at ~R5,000 and Jack's share is R2,000/site, that's
R4,000/month — 26 founding memberships' worth — from month one, with zero
ad spend and zero cold outreach. Upside scales with the friend's sales.

## Kill criteria (so this doesn't become drift)

- Friend not interested, or no first build within 14 days → drop it, no
  hard feelings, back to StoreBrief full-time.
- Jack ends up doing unpaid work → stop immediately; fee agreed in writing
  (even a WhatsApp message) before each build.

## The proposal message (send as-is or in Jack's words)

> Bro — proposal. You're selling sites and ads and your clients pay; I've
> got a build machine (me + AI) that can turn out a full site in a day —
> design, copy, working forms, the lot. You sell like you already do, I
> build, we agree a flat fee per site. Your capacity doubles, quality goes
> up, you keep the client. And every site we ship can include my weekly
> sales-report tool free, which makes your offer stronger. Want to test it
> on one real client this week? If you don't like the result, you pay nothing.
