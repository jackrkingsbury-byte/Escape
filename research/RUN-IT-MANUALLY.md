## Setup with a new customer (once, ~5 minutes)
The customer messages **Sipho's normal WhatsApp number** — nothing changes for them. You just need to be able to reply from it. Use **WhatsApp's linked-devices** feature:

1. Sit with Sipho (or screen-share). On **his** phone: WhatsApp → **Settings → Linked Devices → Link a Device**.
2. On **your** laptop, open **web.whatsapp.com** → it shows a QR code.
3. Sipho scans your laptop's QR with his phone.
4. Done — **Sipho's WhatsApp is now open on your laptop too.** You can see and reply to his enquiries. Replies show up from *his* number.

(If he's not comfortable linking his personal WhatsApp, alternative: he uses **WhatsApp Business** app, or you set up a dedicated number he puts on his ads. Linked device is smoothest.)

## How you reply (the recipe)
When a lead messages, you answer within a minute using this pattern. Keep Claude open in another tab to help you word tricky ones.

**The 4 moves, every time:**
1. **Acknowledge fast + warm.** "Hi! Thanks for reaching out 👋"
2. **Ask the 2–3 things that matter.** What's the job? Where? How urgent?
3. **Give a price *range*** (from Sipho's rates) + reassure a firm quote follows.
4. **Push to book.** Offer a slot / get their address.

**Worked example (geyser):**
- *Customer:* "Do you fix geysers? Mine's leaking"
- *You (as the business):* "Hi! Yes — we handle leaking and burst geysers, including after hours. Whereabouts are you, and is it dripping or fully burst?"
- *Customer:* "Fully burst, Table View"
- *You:* "Got it. A geyser replacement in Table View is usually **R4,500–R7,000** depending on the unit — we'll confirm on site. I can get our plumber to you **8am tomorrow**. Want me to lock it in and send him your address?"
- *Customer:* "Yes please"
- *You:* "Booked ✅ I'll confirm shortly. What's the address, and is there parking out front?"

**Before you start a customer:** get Sipho's price ranges for his 10 most common jobs + his working hours + service areas. Keep them in a note so your replies are accurate.

**Rule:** never invent a firm price — always a *range*, "confirmed on site." If it's weird or big, say "let me get [owner] to confirm the exact quote" and flag it to Sipho.

## Finding customers (do this first)
Target businesses **already paying for leads** (they feel the pain most):
1. Search Google/Facebook for "[plumber / electrician / mobile mechanic] near [your city]" — the ones running **ads** are your list.
2. Aim for **20 businesses**. DM or WhatsApp them.

**Cold message:**
> Hi [name] — I noticed you run ads for [plumbing]. Most businesses like yours lose a few leads a week that message after hours and never get a reply — they book whoever answers first. I built something that instantly replies to every WhatsApp enquiry and books the job for you, 24/7, in your name. Can I show you a 5-min demo? Here's the page: https://escape-nevermiss3.vercel.app

**Follow-up (2 days later):**
> Hey [name] — worth 5 mins? A plumber I set this up for caught a R6,000 geyser job that came in at 9pm. That one job paid for a year. Happy to show you live.

**The close (on the call/chat):** run the geyser demo above live on your phone so they *see* it, then offer the R750 first month.
