# Growth Without Cold Outreach — the honest re-plan

## The constraint we now design around
The founder will not do cold outreach (DMs/calls to strangers). That is not a
character flaw — it is a design constraint, and plans that ignore who the
founder actually is always fail. So the business must use **inbound
distribution: channels where buyers come to you.**

## The three inbound channels that actually work for solo founders (researched)

### 1. Content-led / build-in-public (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts)
- TikTok's For You page surfaces videos by **interest, not follower count** — a
  zero-follower account can reach millions. SMB-focused SaaS is among the
  strongest TikTok-ROI categories in 2026. Expect 2–3 months to consistent
  results. ([Buffer](https://buffer.com/resources/tiktok-marketing/),
  [Hootsuite](https://blog.hootsuite.com/tiktok-marketing/),
  [Metricool](https://metricool.com/tiktok-marketing/))
- **Founder fit: this is the one channel where being 16 is an *advantage*.**
  Native content instinct + the "16-year-old builds an AI business" story is
  itself shareable material. Cold-calling plumbers fights his weakness;
  making videos uses his strength.

### 2. Engineering-as-marketing (free tools that earn attention + SEO)
- Free tools solve a searched-for problem, get shared, earn backlinks, and
  funnel users to the paid product. Documented indie-hacker wins: a free
  side-tool hit the Hacker News front page and top blogs; teams win SEO with
  free AI mini-tools; one maker hit 1k registered users this way.
  ([Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/engineering-as-marketing-theory-and-practice-b1a89d71df),
  [free AI tools for SEO](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/winning-seo-by-building-free-ai-tools-kAG8LZJZ3fu7Pr1RBTeB),
  [1k users](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/hitting-1k-registered-user-with-engineering-as-marketing-358f62aaac))
- Slow but compounding; zero outreach; entirely buildable with Claude.

### 3. Marketplace distribution (Shopify App Store, etc.)
- Real inbound (merchants search + self-install; Shopify bills for you; 0% rev
  share on the first $1M). But: **17,600+ apps**, ~7,000 added since 2024,
  2–4 week review, GraphQL-only + GDPR webhook requirements. Viable, not a
  shortcut. ([Craftberry stats](https://craftberry.co/articles/shopify-app-store-statistics),
  [review process](https://shopify.dev/docs/apps/launch/app-store-review/review-process),
  [requirements](https://shopify.dev/docs/apps/launch/shopify-app-store/app-store-requirements))
- Restart cost: abandons the NeverMiss brand/site/AI engine already built.

## Decision
**Keep NeverMiss. Replace its go-to-market, not the product.** The product was
never the broken part this time — the GTM assumed founder-led cold sales, which
this founder won't do. New GTM = inbound only:

1. **Free tool (built):** the **Missed Call Cost Calculator** at
   `/tools/missed-call-calculator` — a business owner enters missed calls/week,
   close rate, average job value → sees the Rand they lose per month/year.
   Shareable, quantifies the pain, funnels to the live demo. Classic
   engineering-as-marketing.
2. **Content engine (founder's job, 30 days):** short vertical videos:
   - screen-recordings of the live `/demo` answering a burst-geyser enquiry at
     "2am" — "I built an AI receptionist for plumbers, watch it work";
   - build-in-public as a 16-year-old founder (the age *is* the hook);
   - calculator results as content ("this plumber is losing R16k/month").
   Post to TikTok + IG Reels + YouTube Shorts + relevant SA Facebook groups.
   Bio link → site → demo → WhatsApp "Book a demo" (inbound, they message first).
3. **Marketplace (later option):** if content shows demand from online-native
   niches, a Shopify-app wedge remains the documented fallback (see
   OPPORTUNITY-RESEARCH.md) — but only after this GTM is genuinely tried for
   ~60–90 days.

## Success signals (60–90 days)
- One video >10k views, or calculator sessions >200/mo → the channel works.
- Inbound "how much / set this up" messages → the business works.
- Neither after 90 days of consistent posting → revisit the Shopify-app route.
