# What people actually buy — evidence, not opinion

**Date:** 21 July 2026. Written because Jack (correctly) pushed back: "no one's
gonna do all of this… it needs to make people feel they're gonna do well."
He asked for real research, not vibes. Here it is, with sources.

## Finding 1 — People buy a FEELING (transformation), not features

- People don't buy features; they buy the "after state" — a better version of
  themselves. Products that promise an emotional change *within minutes* get
  bought on impulse, not deliberation.
  ([sellyourservice](https://blog.sellyourservice.co.uk/2020/11/people-buy-transformation-and-enthusiasm/),
  [aimforbehavior](https://www.aimforbehavior.com/articles/behavioral-science-courses-2023-4ygj4))
- **Verdict on StoreBrief:** "6 metrics in plain English" is a *feature*. Jack's
  instinct is right — it has to sell the feeling ("your store is winning / you'll
  do well"), not the report. We repositioned toward this, but it's still a tool,
  not a transformation.

## Finding 2 — What beginners actually sell (and it's low-ticket, instant)

- Best-sellers for beginners: templates, ebooks, AI-prompt packs, printables,
  mini-courses. **$10–$99.** No inventory, instant delivery, high margin.
  ([optinmonster](https://optinmonster.com/digital-products/),
  [sellfy](https://sellfy.com/blog/digital-products/))

## Finding 3 — The brutal number nobody posts on Instagram

- **Median Gumroad creator earns $72/month. 44% of all products make exactly $0.**
  The sellers who made real money ($15k) "had existing audiences."
  ([Low Content Profits](https://lowcontentprofits.com/gumroad-digital-products-earnings/))
- **This is the whole game.** It's not the product. It's distribution. The people
  who make money already had people to sell to.

## Finding 4 — Low-friction wins, and shareable outputs pull people in free

- Every minute between "arrive" and "first value" is a drop-off. Winners
  (Calendly) give instant value free and let the product spread itself.
- Tools that produce a **shareable/public output** (a report, a result) bring
  users in **without paid ads.**
  ([ProductLed](https://productled.com/blog/product-led-growth-definition),
  [Advertising Week](https://advertisingweek.com/to-successfully-implement-a-product-led-strategy-focus-on-creating-a-friction-free-user-experience/))

## The honest conclusion

Jack is right that StoreBrief has too much friction and sells a feature, not a
feeling. **But the research also kills the hope that a *different* product fixes
this.** Median seller: $72/mo. 44%: zero. The single variable that separates
winners from the 44% is **distribution — having people to sell to.** No product
choice, no landing-page rewrite, no new category escapes that.

We have proven this the hard way: StoreBrief is a genuinely good, working product
with **zero** people looking at it. That's not a product bug. That's the bug.

## What actually follows from the evidence (for Jack's real situation)

Three traits the winners share — score honestly:

| Trait | StoreBrief | A digital product on his own store |
|---|---|---|
| Zero buyer friction | ❌ (they connect a store) | ✅ (click → Yoco → done) |
| Sells a feeling/transformation | ⚠️ improving | ✅ if the product is a transformation |
| Has distribution | ❌ | ❌ |

Both fail on the same box. **Distribution is the job now — not the product.**

The evidence-backed move for someone with no audience and no budget:
1. Pick the thing with **zero buyer friction** and a **strong feeling** — that is a
   low-ticket digital "transformation" product on the Shopify+Yoco he already has,
   OR StoreBrief's free scanner as a *shareable* hook.
2. **Do the distribution.** Post the shareable output. Show 10 real people. There
   is no version of this where money arrives without that step. The $72 median
   proves it.

Enthusiasm is itself a sales factor (people buy from the excited person). So the
tie-breaker on *which* product is: **which one will Jack actually talk about every
day?** That matters more than the category.
